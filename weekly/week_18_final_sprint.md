## Why this week matters

You're interview-ready by Monday of this week. The goal is polish, not new content. Over-learning in the final week breeds anxiety; under-learning breeds regression. Target: 2 hrs/day of deliberate review + 1 full-length end-to-end mock on Wednesday.

**No new topics this week. No new projects. If you feel an urge to dive into something new, that's anxiety — route it into flashcard review instead.**

## Daily breakdown

> **Pace check:** No reading this week. This is active review only: flashcards, recorded STAR stories, one mock. If you feel the urge to read something new, that's anxiety not curiosity — route it into flashcard review instead.

### Mon (2.5 hrs) — Flashcard consolidation + STAR rehearsal
- 90 min: Merge all phase flashcards into `final_review_deck.md`. Aim for 250 cards. Delete duplicates.
- 60 min: Record all 8 STAR stories in their 2-min version. Listen back. One pass of edits.

### Tue (2.5 hrs) — System design polish + resume update
- 90 min: Re-run Week 13 Pattern 1 (real-time analytics) and Week 14 Pattern 2 (cost-constrained rec-feed) cold. Time them.
- 60 min: Update resume. Add P1, P2, P3, P5 (+ P4 as "architecture spec" if applicable). Each line: action verb → what → impact with numbers.

### Wed (3 hrs) — Full-length mock + LinkedIn post #1
- 2 hrs: **Full-length final mock.** 1 behavioral + 1 system design + 1 SQL. Use Pramp or a peer. Get honest feedback.
- 60 min: Write + publish **LinkedIn post #1** — a technical post about one of your projects. 400 words. Architecture diagram or query. Suggested topic: "Why we moved 250 TB to Apache Iceberg — 3 things I learned."

### Thu (2.5 hrs) — Gap closing + LinkedIn post #2
- 90 min: Close any gaps from Wednesday's mock. Usually 1 specific technical area + 1 story delivery issue.
- 60 min: Write + publish **LinkedIn post #2** — a perspective post. 400 words. Suggested topic: "The 4 questions I'd ask before picking a streaming engine in 2026" or "What I wish I'd known about Kafka consumers before running 40 of them in production."

### Fri (3 hrs) — Final review + rest
- 60 min: Final flashcard pass — 250 cards, ~30 seconds each, any gap marked for morning-of-interview review
- 60 min: Read STAR stories one final time. Quiet, calm, no notes in mouth.
- 60 min: **Close the project folder. Take the weekend off. Start interviewing Monday Aug 10.**

## Interview-morning ritual (for reference starting Aug 10)

- 15 min before: One flashcard pass on the specific area you know is weakest
- 5 min before: Review the company's recent engineering blog posts — often interviewers recycle topics from there
- During: Breathe. Clarify before answering. Take notes visible to them.
- After: Journal what was asked. Patterns repeat.
